About Me

Hi if you haven't seen by now my name is Francisco Soltero. I am a junior at Tulane University where I am studying Computer Science and Mathematics. I was born and raised in San Diego, California but my parents were originally from Puerto Rico and Argentina. I alwasy got the chance to go back to Argentina and get to see my family. All this being when I came to Tulane it was a big change but I felt I had the tools to deal with it because of how I was raised and where I am from. Getting to my freshman year dorm originally thinking I was going to go into a pre med route but instead it was my sister who called me and really encouraged me to give programming and computer science a try because she knew what type of person I was. I took her advice and took my first programming class and I thought at first that I was way out of place because I had never programmed prior to taking the class. I remember getting my first lab 2 weeks after learning what python was and it was to complete a partially completed wordle in python. The project took me a whole week because I was stuck on getting the keys to change color based on the correctness of the letters. But I remember the feeling of when I finally cracked the lab and from that day forward I couldn't imagine myself doing anything else. I then built my own text based adventure game with help from my professor, but then I turned it into its own application where using the JFrame library in java. After that I also built a MIPS dissasembler in java that can translate binary code into readable mips code.
